About me

I am postdoctoral researcher in the Budker Group at Johannes Gutenberg University in Mainz, Germany. My research is currently centered around the development of highly sensitive magnetometers based on atomic vapors or defects in diamonds, and using these quantum devices to detect magnetic fields within the human body. Collaborating with medical doctors, we are searching for ways to make these exciting technologies practical and useful in mapping brain function, assisting neurosurgery, and detecting neurological disease.

While my passion is, unsurprisingly, experimental physics, you may occasionally find me, playing the banjo, climbing rocks or perhaps practising Capoeira.

Lyka Sethi and I got married at City Hall in September 2017!
