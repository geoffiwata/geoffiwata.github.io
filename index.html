About me

I am an AMO physics graduate student in the Columbia University Physics department under Prof. Tanya Zelevinsky. My research is currently centered around developing a new way to cool and trap molecules, using a combination of cryogenic techniques and lasers.

I enjoy living in NYC, experiencing the pace, the people, and most importantly, the food. Living here has cemented my firm belief in the importance of thinking beyond our own self-interests and of looking out for those around us, whether they are old friends or strangers in passing. While my passion is, unsurprisingly, experimental physics, you may occasionally find me woodworking, playing the banjo, climbing rocks or perhaps practising Capoeira.
